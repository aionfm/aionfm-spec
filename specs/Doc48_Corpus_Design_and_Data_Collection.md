# Document 48 – Corpus Design & Data Collection

## Purpose

This document defines the domains and sources for building the training corpus of AionFM.  A diverse, high‑quality corpus is essential for pretraining a foundation model that generalizes across domains.  The document provides guidelines for data collection, cleaning, anonymization and metadata management.

## Audience

Data engineers, researchers and data stewards responsible for assembling the training corpus.

## Domain Coverage

To enable cross‑domain transfer, the corpus should include time series from a broad set of domains:

* **Retail and e‑commerce:** sales, prices, promotions, returns, inventory levels.
* **Energy and utilities:** electricity load, generation, grid frequency, weather, gas consumption.
* **Industrial telemetry:** sensor readings (temperature, vibration, pressure), equipment throughput, maintenance logs.
* **Healthcare operations:** patient arrivals, admissions, bed occupancy, procedure volumes.
* **Logistics and mobility:** shipment volumes, traffic speeds, fleet utilisation, passenger demand.
* **Finance and economics:** asset prices, volumes, volatility, macro‑economic indicators.
* **Web and digital services:** website traffic, user engagement, latency metrics.
* **Climate and environment:** temperature, precipitation, wind, air quality, water levels.

## Data Sources

* **Public datasets:** open datasets such as UCI, M4, M5, electricity load datasets, weather archives, sensor benchmarks and economic indicators.
* **Partner contributions:** curated datasets from partner organisations willing to share anonymized data.
* **Synthetic data:** augment the corpus with synthetic series generated by the synthetic trajectory generator (Doc15) to cover rare regimes and edge cases.

## Collection Guidelines

1. **Legal and ethical compliance:** ensure all data collection complies with licensing agreements and privacy regulations.  Obtain consent where required.
2. **Data diversity:** sample across geography, seasonality, scale, frequency and domain to avoid over‑representation of any single pattern.
3. **Metadata retention:** preserve metadata (entity identifiers, covariate descriptions, domain labels) to support domain‑specific training and retrieval.
4. **Temporal granularity:** include series at various frequencies (minutely, hourly, daily, weekly, monthly).  Align time zones and timestamps.
5. **Anonymization:** remove or obfuscate personally identifiable information and company‑specific identifiers.  For sensitive domains, aggregate or mask data to protect privacy.
6. **Normalization and units:** record units and ensure consistency (e.g. kWh vs MWh).  Provide transformation metadata for reversible normalization.
7. **Quality checks:** detect and remove corrupted segments, extreme outliers and inconsistent sampling; flag missing values and unusual spikes.

## Storage and Versioning

* Use columnar formats (Parquet, Arrow) for efficient storage and streaming.  Partition data by domain and frequency.
* Maintain versioned snapshots of the corpus.  Document changes and additions with timestamps and source information.
* Use metadata catalogs (e.g. DataHub) to index datasets and facilitate retrieval.

## Ethical Considerations

* **Bias awareness:** monitor domain representation to avoid biases toward wealthy or accessible regions; ensure inclusion of under‑served populations.
* **Transparency:** document sources, data transformations and limitations to foster trust and reproducibility.
* **Consent:** when using sensitive data (e.g. health records), ensure consent and anonymization are handled properly.

## Corpus Governance

* Establish a **corpus governance committee** that reviews data sources, monitors privacy compliance and approves additions to the corpus.
* Provide a **corpus usage policy** that outlines who can access which parts of the corpus and under what conditions.
